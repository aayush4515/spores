Small circles. Steady support.

A private, calm companion for the people you trust.

Team Name · Hackathon 2026

The problem

We're bad at noticing.

group_off 60% of people wouldn't know if a close friend was struggling
volume_up Group chats are noisy. Social media is public. Therapy apps are clinical.
search_off No tool exists for small, private circles to gently care for each other
The gap: intelligent, calm support between "I'm fine" and "I need help"
Our solution

What Spore does

mood

Gentle check-ins

Soft mood signals — Good, Okay, Not great. Not forced daily logs or journals.

monitoring

Activity intelligence

Behavioral patterns surface quiet struggles. Privacy-safe: "inactive today" — never raw data.

auto_awesome

Smart prompts

"Neel has been inactive and missed check-ins" — with one-tap care actions.

Spore notices what group chats can't.

Under the hood

Tech + Design

Technical architecture

data_object 5-factor health scoring — check-in × activity × signals × recency × engagement
trending_up 4-tier support severity with automatic activity-based escalation
shield Privacy layer — all raw data becomes qualitative labels only
React 18 · Vite · Context API · Hand-crafted CSS design system

UX principles

spa Calm > Urgent — no red alerts, no notification counts
format_quote Qualitative > Quantitative — "Growing warmth" not "50/100"
volunteer_activism Optional > Mandatory — every prompt says "at your pace"
Where this goes

Impact + Roadmap

Target: Families with aging parents, close friend groups, caregiving circles, university wellness.
Gap: Nothing sits between Life360 (surveillance) and group chats (chaos).

Go to market V1

  • End-to-end encrypted cloud sync
  • Push notifications for support prompts
  • Wearable / phone sensor integration (with consent)
  • Onboarding + invite flow

Future vision V2

  • AI-personalized prompt timing and tone
  • Spore health trends over weeks
  • Crisis escalation to emergency contacts
  • Community templates (grief, elder care, new parents)
